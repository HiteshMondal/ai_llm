## Productivity & Time Management

### Tips for Getting Things Done
- Break large tasks into smaller, actionable steps.
- Use the "2-minute rule": if a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately.
- Prioritize tasks using the Eisenhower Matrix:
  - Urgent + Important → Do it now
  - Not Urgent + Important → Schedule it
  - Urgent + Not Important → Delegate it
  - Not Urgent + Not Important → Eliminate it
- Review your to-do list at the start and end of each day.
- Batch similar tasks together to reduce context switching.

### Focus Techniques
- Pomodoro Technique: Work for 25 minutes, take a 5-minute break. After 4 cycles, take a longer break (15–30 minutes).
- Time blocking: Reserve specific time slots in your calendar for deep work.
- Eliminate distractions: Silence notifications, use focus mode, keep your workspace tidy.

## Health & Wellness

### Physical Health Basics
- Aim for 7–9 hours of sleep per night for most adults.
- Drink at least 8 glasses (2 liters) of water daily, more if physically active.
- Regular exercise: At least 150 minutes of moderate activity per week (e.g., brisk walking, cycling).
- Eat a balanced diet: include vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats.
- Limit processed foods, excess sugar, and alcohol.
- Take regular breaks from screens — follow the 20-20-20 rule: every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds.

### Mental Health Basics
- Practice mindfulness or meditation even for just 5–10 minutes a day.
- Stay socially connected — talk to friends, family, or colleagues regularly.
- Journaling can help process emotions and reduce stress.
- Seek professional help if you experience persistent anxiety, depression, or burnout.
- It is okay to say no — protecting your energy is important.

## Learning & Personal Growth

### How to Learn Effectively
- Active recall: Test yourself instead of just rereading. Use flashcards or practice problems.
- Spaced repetition: Review material at increasing intervals over time.
- Teach what you learn: Explaining a concept to someone else is one of the best ways to understand it deeply (Feynman Technique).
- Take notes in your own words, not verbatim copy.
- Focus on one topic at a time — multitasking while learning reduces retention.

### Building Good Habits
- Start small: Make the new habit easy enough that you cannot fail on day one.
- Attach new habits to existing ones (habit stacking): e.g., "After I make coffee, I will read for 10 minutes."
- Track your streak — consistency is more important than intensity early on.
- Reward yourself for milestones to reinforce the behavior.
- If you miss a day, never miss twice — one missed day is a mistake, two is a new habit forming.

## Finance & Money Basics

### Budgeting
- The 50/30/20 rule is a simple starting point:
  - 50% of income → needs (rent, food, utilities)
  - 30% → wants (entertainment, dining out)
  - 20% → savings and debt repayment
- Track your spending for at least one month to understand where your money goes.
- Build an emergency fund — aim for 3–6 months of living expenses saved.

### Saving & Investing (General Principles)
- Pay yourself first: transfer savings automatically as soon as you receive income.
- Avoid lifestyle inflation — as income grows, increase savings before spending more.
- Compound interest is powerful — starting early matters more than the amount invested.
- Diversify investments — do not put all money in one place.
- Note: This assistant does not provide personalized financial advice. Consult a qualified financial advisor for decisions specific to your situation.

## Technology & Everyday Tech Help

### Common Computer Tips
- Restart your device before assuming something is broken — it fixes most issues.
- Keep software and operating systems updated for security and performance.
- Use strong, unique passwords for every account. Use a password manager.
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on important accounts.
- Back up important files regularly — follow the 3-2-1 rule:
  - 3 copies of data, on 2 different media types, with 1 copy offsite or in the cloud.

### Internet & Online Safety
- Do not click links in unsolicited emails or messages — verify the sender first.
- Check website URLs carefully before entering passwords or payment info.
- Use a VPN on public Wi-Fi networks.
- Be cautious about what personal information you share online.
- If something online seems too good to be true, it usually is.

## Communication & Writing

### Writing Clearly
- Start with your main point — do not bury the conclusion.
- Use short sentences and paragraphs. Aim for one idea per paragraph.
- Avoid unnecessary words: "due to the fact that" → "because".
- Read your writing out loud to catch awkward phrasing.
- Always proofread before sending anything important.

### Email Best Practices
- Use a clear, specific subject line.
- State the purpose of the email in the first sentence.
- Keep it concise — if it takes more than 30 seconds to read, consider trimming it.
- End with a clear call to action: what do you need the recipient to do?
- Reply within 24 hours when possible.

## Problem Solving

### A Simple Framework
1. Define the problem clearly — what exactly is wrong?
2. Identify the root cause — ask "why?" five times if needed (5 Whys method).
3. Brainstorm possible solutions — list them without judging yet.
4. Evaluate options — consider effort, cost, and impact.
5. Choose and implement the best option.
6. Review the outcome — did it solve the problem? What can be improved?

### When Stuck
- Step away and come back with fresh eyes — sleep on it if possible.
- Talk it through with someone else — explaining the problem often reveals the solution.
- Search for how others have solved similar problems.
- Simplify: strip the problem down to its most basic form.

## Frequently Asked General Questions

Q: What is the best way to start a new habit?
A: Start extremely small — smaller than you think necessary. Attach it to an existing habit. Track it daily. Miss one day, never two.

Q: How do I improve my focus and stop procrastinating?
A: Break the task into the smallest possible first step. Start a timer for just 5 minutes. Most procrastination comes from starting — once begun, continuing is easier.

Q: How do I stay motivated long term?
A: Motivation fades — systems and routines beat motivation. Set clear goals, build habits that make progress automatic, and track small wins.

Q: What should I do when I feel overwhelmed?
A: Stop and write everything down — get it out of your head. Then pick just one thing to work on next. Focus on one task at a time.

Q: How do I make better decisions?
A: Gather enough information to be informed but not paralyzed. Consider the reversibility — easy to undo decisions can be made faster. Think about your future self: will this decision matter in 5 years?

Q: How can I improve my sleep?
A: Keep a consistent sleep schedule (same time every day, including weekends). Avoid screens 30–60 minutes before bed. Keep your bedroom cool and dark. Avoid caffeine after 2 PM.
